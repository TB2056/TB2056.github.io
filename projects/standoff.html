Standoff

Augmented Reality Game made in Unity with Vuforia

Standoff is a Western-themed augmented reality game consisting with both a physical board game and digital minigames made in Unity. In short, 4 players would move around on a physical board while drawing cards. Players can battle each other by scanning their cards with their computer’s webcam to play one of 4 minigames, with different effects depending on the type of card from their deck they scanned. Whoever gets the highest score wins after a timer expires wins the battle.

Standoff uses Vuforia to detect physical cards, and each card corresponds to one of 4 minigames, with the suite and rank of the card affecting the gameplay significantly.

The game was made as a group project for GIMM 110 in about 3-4 weeks. There were 4 people total in our group, with each person responsible for a different aspect of the game. I was primarily responsible for programming augmented reality functionality, as well as programming one of the game's minigames and creating Standoff's introduction animation using Adobe Animate.
