About Us!

What is Open House Games?

Open House Games is a small game studio that started out as a one-man project by Thomas Brown which started Open House while at Boise State University while studying Games, Interactive Mobile Media. As someone who was always interested in both the technical and artistic side of games, he quickly developed a passion for the game development process.

From the beginning of his career, Thomas was fascinated with what independent developers and modders were able to accomplish with few resources and pure grit and passion. This is what inspired the creation of Open House Games. With help from the community, Open House aims to bring a new perspective to the game development industry and showcase the value of community involvement in game development.

Why Open House Games?

There are three main reasons why we are committed to Open House Games:

1. Empowering the community helps our games.

We feel a lot of game companies suffer from a lack of community involvement. Designing games is often tricky business, and it can be hard to tell what is actually working or what people are even interested in. We want to give people a chance to voice their opinions and help us make the best games we can.

2. We want to introduce more people to game development.

Furthermore, we also hope to give opportunities for promising game developers to learn with first hand experience from professionals how to develop and design games. We host game jams, interns, and more to help people learn this amazing and sometimes difficult artform.

3. We just love making games.

Need we say more?

If what we've said here so far has piqued your interest, feel free to take a look at any of our projects, or event join the development process of any of our games! From joining a game jam, applying for our team, or event just suggesting any improvements for one of our games, we'd love to have you and see what you can do!
